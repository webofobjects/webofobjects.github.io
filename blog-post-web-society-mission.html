Why My Mission Is to Invent a Web Operating System & a Web Society

How crazy does it sound to say my mission is to invent a Web Operating System and a Web Society?

It might feel big, unclear, or hard to imagine — I get that.

But we forget something important: We take the web for granted.

Tim Berners-Lee — the inventor of the World Wide Web — has said many times that today’s web is not what he imagined.

1. He wanted an open, decentralized web — not one controlled by giant platforms.

Yet a few tech companies dominate everything, from what we see to where our data lives.

2. He never expected today’s level of surveillance.

The web wasn’t meant to track users, sell data, or power mass monitoring.

3. He imagined collaboration, not endless scrolling.

His first browser let you read and edit pages. Today the web is mostly “read-only.”

4. He didn’t foresee misinformation and online toxicity.

Viral lies and algorithmic polarization weren’t part of the plan.

5. He didn’t expect commercialization to reshape the culture of the web.

Ads, paywalls, and engagement-driven design now define most online experiences.

“The web was supposed to help humanity, but it has been hijacked by forces that undermine its goals.”

So I dare you: be curious.

Give my ideas a chance.

Subscribe to my YouTube channel.

Share our message if you want to help.

Because when the world finally realizes the web can be more than pages and algorithms, you’ll be able to say:

“I was there at the beginning. I helped build the Web Society.”
